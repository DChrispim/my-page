Academic Formation

Degree Resume
PhD in Physics - Universidade do Estado do Rio de Janeiro (2017 - 2022)
  • Thesis: Weyl superconductor with dynamical pseudo-axion field, an interplay between high energy and condensed matter physics
  • Summary: Topological materials require a new characterization called topological order, which connects different phases of matter that are not distinguishable via symmetry considerations. Such materials have an electromagnetic response rooted in the so-called theta term, which is analogous to CFJ couplings (Weyl semimetal) and axion-like field (topological insulator). In this work, the fundamentals of topological materials were examined, their description within field theory, as well as obtaining the electromagnetic response of a semimetal when perturbed by a quartic fermionic pairing, triggering the formation of a charged chiral condensate, resulting in an axionic superconductor (massive photons interacting with axion-like particles). Additionally, 1-loop quantum corrections generated by axionic fluctuations to the Yukawa potential and modifications to the London penetration depth were analyzed.
  • Keywords: Topological Materials; Quantum Field Theory; Condensed Matter; Superconductor.
Master's degree in Physics - Universidade do Estado do Rio de Janeiro (2015-2017)
  • Master's thesis: Boundary conditions in AdS CFT: scalar and vector fields
  • Summary: AdS/CFT (anti-De Sitter/Conformal Field Theory) is a holographic relationship between two theories, one with gravity in a universe with d dimensions and another conformal field theory defined on the boundary of this space with (d-1) dimensions. In this work, an introduction to anti-De Sitter spacetime and conformal field theories was provided, along with a comprehensive approach to different boundary conditions of spacetime using scalar and massive vector field theories, and calculating the 2-point function for the cases studied.
  • Keywords: AdS/CFT correspondence; Holographic ; Quantum Field Theory; Boundary Conditions.
Bachelor degree in Physics - Universidade do Estado do Rio de Janeiro (2010-2015)
  • Monograph: A brief study of Topologic Isolators: an approach to the TME
  • Summary: Study of the topological-magnetic effect (TME) by examining the topological term in the electromagnetic Lagrangian. Calculation of constitutive equations and development of the problem of image charges.
  • Keywords: Topological Insulator; Electromagnetism; Topological-Magnetic Effect; Magnetic monopoles.
